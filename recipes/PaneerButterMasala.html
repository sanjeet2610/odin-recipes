Paneer Butter Masala

About Paneer Butter Masala

Paneer Butter Masala, also known as Butter Paneer, is a rich and creamy tomato-based curry made with paneer (Indian cottage cheese), butter, cream, and a blend of aromatic spices. This vegetarian delight is one of the most popular dishes in Indian restaurants and is best served with naan, roti, or jeera rice.

Ingredients :

For the gravy base :

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 3-4 cloves
  • 1-2 green cardamoms
  • 1 inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 medium onion (roughly chopped)
  • 3-4 medium tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon chopped garlic
  • 1 tablespoon chopped ginger
  • 8-10 cashew nuts
  • Salt to taste

Spices :

  • teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • ½ teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves), crushed

Others :

  • 200-250 grams paneer (cut into cubes)
  • 2-3 tablespoons fresh cream
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • Water as needed

Prepration Step :

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon oil and 1 tablespoon butter in a pan.
  2. Add bay leaf, cloves, cardamoms, and cinnamon stick; sauté for 30 seconds
  3. Add chopped onions, garlic, and ginger. Cook until onions turn golden.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cashew nuts. Cook until tomatoes are soft and mushy.
  5. Remove the bay leaf, allow the mixture to cool slightly, and blend into a smooth paste.
  6. In the same pan, heat 1 tablespoon butter and pour in the blended paste.
  7. Cook the paste on medium heat until it thickens and releases oil from the sides.
  8. Add red chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  9. Add water to adjust the gravy consistency as desired.
  10. Add paneer cubes and stir gently to coat them in the masala.
  11. Add garam masala and crushed kasuri methi.
  12. Pour in fresh cream, mix gently, and simmer for 2-3 minutes.
  13. Garnish with more cream and fresh coriander leaves.
  14. Serve hot with naan, roti, or jeera rice.

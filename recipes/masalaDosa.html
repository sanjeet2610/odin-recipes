Masala Dosa

About Masala Dosa

While masala dosa is a famous snack most popular in South India, these tasty stuffed crepes are enjoyed in North India as well and can be found on menus throughout the world.

Masala dosa are crispy, soft, savory and healthy crepes made with a rice and lentil batter. The dosa are gluten-free and dairy-free, with a sturdy yet soft texture that’s great for holding a hearty homemade potato filling.

Ingredients :

For Dosa Batter :

  • Rice - 2 cups (usually raw rice or parboiled rice)
  • Urad dal (black gram) - ½ cup
  • Fenugreek seeds (methi) - ½ tsp
  • Water as needed
  • Salt - to taste
  • Oil or ghee - for cooking

For Potato Masala (Stuffing) :

  • Boiled potatoes - 3 to 4 (medium-sized)
  • Onions - 1 to 2 (thinly sliced)
  • Green chilies - 1 to 2 (finely chopped)
  • Ginger - 1 inch (grated or finely chopped)
  • Mustard seeds - ½ tsp
  • Chana dal (split chickpeas) - 1 tbsp (optional, for texture)
  • Curry leaves - a few
  • Turmeric powder - ¼ tsp
  • Salt - to taste
  • Oil -1 to 2 tbsp
  • Coriander leaves
  • Lemon juice

Served With :

  • Coconut chutney
  • Sambar

Prepration Steps :

  1. Soak and ferment the batter: Soak rice, urad dal, and poha separately in water for 4 to 6 hours. After soaking, grind them together with water to make a smooth batter. Add salt and keep the batter to ferment overnight or for 8 to 12 hours until it rises and becomes airy.
  2. Prepare the potato masala: Boil potatoes, peel them, and mash them. In a pan, heat oil and add mustard seeds. Once they splutter, add urad dal, chopped green chilies, curry leaves, and a pinch of hing. Then add sliced onions and sauté until golden. Add turmeric powder and the mashed potatoes. Mix well, cook for a few minutes, and turn off the flame. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves if desired.
  3. Make the dosa: Heat a tawa and pour a ladle of fermented batter in the center. Spread it out evenly in a circular motion to make a thin layer. Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges and cook until it turns golden and crispy.
  4. Add the filling and fold: Place a portion of the potato masala in the center of the dosa. Fold the dosa over the filling and press gently.
  5. Serve: Serve hot with coconut chutney, tomato chutney, and sambar for a complete South Indian meal.
